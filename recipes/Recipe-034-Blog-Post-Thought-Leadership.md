## The Problem

You have expertise and ideas to share, but the time to write is always squeezed. Staring at a blank page is intimidating. When you do write, it takes forever to structure your thoughts, find the right hook, and craft a compelling narrative. Content marketing demands consistency, but quality takes time you don't have.

**Pain Points:**
- Blank page paralysis
- Ideas but no time to write
- Inconsistent publishing schedule
- Good content that doesn't engage readers
- Too much time drafting and revising
- Voice and quality vary across pieces

---

## The Outcome

Well-structured articles and blog posts created in a fraction of the time. Content that's engaging, authoritative, and aligned with your voice. A consistent output that builds your thought leadership position without consuming your schedule.

**What You'll Have When Done:**
- Compelling headline options
- Engaging opening that hooks readers
- Well-structured body with clear arguments
- Supporting evidence and examples
- Strong conclusion with takeaways
- SEO-optimized content (if needed)

---

## When to Use This Recipe

**Good Fit:**
- Company blog posts

**Not a Good Fit:**

- Why should they care?
- What action do I want them to take (even if just "think differently")?

A clear thesis makes everything else easier.

### Step 4: Review and Refine
**Time: 20-30 minutes**

Review the draft for:

**Message:**
- Does it deliver on the headline's promise?
- Is the core thesis clear throughout?
- Does it add value for the reader?

**Voice:**
- Does it sound like you?
- Is the tone right for the audience?
- Any phrases that feel off?

**Evidence:**
- Are claims supported?
- Do examples resonate?
- Anything that needs fact-checking?

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Content feels generic | Insufficient differentiation | Add: "Include my specific perspective/experience that makes this unique" |
| Too long and wandering | No clear structure | Ask: "Tighten this to [X] words. Cut anything that doesn't serve the thesis" |
| Doesn't sound like me | Voice not captured | Add: "Here's a sample of my writing: [paste]. Match this voice" |
| Weak opening | Boring lead | Ask: "Rewrite the opening to hook the reader immediately. Start with surprise or tension" |
| No clear takeaway | Thesis buried | Ask: "Make the main point crystal clear. What should readers remember?" |
| Too academic | Wrong tone for audience | Ask: "Make this more conversational. Write like you're explaining to a smart colleague" |

## Tips from Experience

1. **Start with the headline.** A great headline clarifies your thinking. If you can't summarize the piece in 10 words, you don't know what you're writing.

2. **Hook them fast.** You have 2-3 sentences to earn attention. Don't waste them on preamble.

3. **One idea, well developed.** Trying to cover too much dilutes everything. Pick one core message and go deep.

4. **Tell stories.** Data informs. Stories persuade. Combine both.

5. **Write for skimmers.** Most people will skim. Subheadings, short paragraphs, and pull quotes ensure they still get value.

6. **End strong.** A weak conclusion undermines everything. Give readers a clear takeaway, call to action, or memorable final thought.

For educational content:
```

Topic: How to [achieve outcome]

Desired outcome: [What they want to achieve]

Structure as:
1. Why this matters
2. Step-by-step instructions
```

For provocative thought leadership:
```

Tone: Confident, backed by evidence, not arrogant
Goal: Make readers rethink assumptions
```
